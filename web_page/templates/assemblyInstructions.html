Assembly Instructions

Introduction

The kit is composed of an insole and the hardware assembly that will be attached to the insole. The hardware assembly consists of a vibration motor, force sensing unit, and inertial measurement unit (IMU). Throughout the lessons, the kit will be used for various activities related to the topics in the video lessons.

Learning Objectives

Students will learn how to assemble their kit to prepare for future activities, as well as the different devices used and their purpose.

Materials

×

Hardware Assembly

The hardware assembly includes a vibration motor, a force sensing unit converting mechanical force into electrical signals, and an inertial measurement unit (IMU) which includes a combination of sensors like accelerometers and gyroscopes. These sensors will be used to measure foot pressure and movement during activities.

×

Logic Board

The logic board is a printed circuit board (PCB) that controls the components in the hardware assembly. It processes signals from the force sensing unit and IMU and controls the vibration motors.

Step 1

Determine the correct size by aligning your foot on the guide to see which number corresponds with your foot length.

Step 2

Trim the insole based off the size determined in Step 1

Step 3

Lay the hardware onto the insole.

Step 4

Ensure that the vibration motors are aligned in the proper position at the bottom on the insole.

Step 5

Fold the top wire as needed to fit the force sensing unit within the insole

Step 6

Tape the hardware assembly to the insole.

Step 7: Testing!
